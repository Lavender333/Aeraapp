# AERA Role Manual: First Responder

Role: `FIRST_RESPONDER`

## 1) Role Purpose

First Responders use AERA to receive structured household risk signals, prioritize field actions, and report verified status updates during active incidents.

## 2) Core Responsibilities

- Review incoming safety and needs signals
- Prioritize critical households by vulnerability and access constraints
- Confirm ground-truth status updates from field activity
- Coordinate handoffs with org and regional leadership

## 3) Hands-On Workflow

### Step A: Intake and Prioritization

1. Open current incident operational view
2. Identify highest-priority unresolved needs
3. Build response order using severity + accessibility factors

**Pass condition:** trainee can justify top 3 priorities clearly.

### Step B: Field Update Discipline

1. Mark status updates only when verified
2. Distinguish unknown vs confirmed-safe vs confirmed-danger
3. Escalate blocked access conditions promptly

**Pass condition:** updates are accurate, timestamped, and actionable.

### Step C: Handoff to Recovery

1. Summarize unresolved critical cases
2. Transfer active needs to recovery coordination
3. Flag repeat-failure areas for next operational cycle

**Pass condition:** no critical case is orphaned in transition.

## 4) Common Mistakes to Prevent

- Marking assumptions as verified outcomes
- Under-prioritizing medical or transport-limited households
- Delayed escalation when local response is blocked

## 5) Training Completion Check

Responder passes when they can:

- Prioritize correctly from incoming signals
- Maintain verified status integrity
- Execute clean response-to-recovery handoff
